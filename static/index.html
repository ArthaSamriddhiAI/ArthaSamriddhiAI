The Three-Layer Mental Model

              
Any system that allows agents to act under uncertainty must separate three questions without collapsing them:

              

                

                  
1

                  
Evidence Layer

                  
"What does the system believe about the world?" Market observations, risk estimates, regime inference, model-derived outputs with uncertainty. ML belongs here as evidence generation, not authority. Produces beliefs, not decisions.

                

                

                  
2

                  
Governance Layer

                  
"What actions are permitted right now?" The control plane. Encodes mandates, constraints, policies, limits. Deterministic, executable, versioned. Real-time permissioning, not retrospective compliance.

                

                

                  
3

                  
Accountability Layer

                  
"Who acted, under what authority, and how is it recorded?" Preserves institutional memory. Every action has a clear initiator, is linked to contemporaneous evidence, is evaluated under rules in force at the time. Causal lineage, not just logs.

                

              

            


            
            

              
Governance Pipeline Flow

              

                Intent→
                Evidence Compute & Freeze→
                Supervisor Dispatch→
                Agent Consultation Loop→
                Rule Engine (Deterministic)→
                Permission Filter→
                Decision Record
              

              
The orchestrator has bounded authority: cannot place trades, cannot override rules, cannot invent objectives.

            


            
            

              
Cross-Cutting Design Principles

              

                
Agents surface risk; they do NOT decide
Decision authority is always explicit and human.

                
Synthesis ≠ Decision
Aggregation layers clarify the landscape; they don't remove responsibility.

                
Governance is real-time permissioning
Not retrospective compliance. Deterministic, versioned, executable.

                
Decision telemetry > model telemetry
The system of record captures human judgment and intent.

                
Learning is governed and explicit
No silent drift. Judgment evolves like policy: slowly, versioned, with traceability.

                
Off-system judgment is a feature
Humans may incorporate experience and discretion no model can encode.

                
Agentic ≠ Autonomous
Agents coordinate reasoning under explicit mandates.

                
The instant a decision is made, it becomes history
A serious system must reason across both present and past states.
